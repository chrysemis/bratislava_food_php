  • Marjoram:
    This culinary herb is from the same family as oregano. Indeed, it is called oregano in the USA, and the two are often confused. There are several varieties of marjoram; all have different-sized leaves that are green, yellow or variegated. Although all marjoram varieties are fragrant and taste delicate when cooked, each has a different flavour depending on the climate in which it has been grown.
  • Caraway seeds:
    Caraway (Carum carvi), also known as meridian fennel or Persian cumin.
  • Bryndza:
    Bryndza is a sheep cheese milk characteristic strong smell and taste. The cheese is white, tangy, crumbly and slightly moist. It has characteristic odor and flavor with a notable taste of butyric acid.
  • Kefir:
    Drink made from settled milk, slightly similar to buttermilk but sharper taste.
  • Horalky:
    Horalky is a biscuit that is so popular that it has been adopted as something of national food. It is one of the meals we miss most when away of home!
    The base is several layers of biscuit filled with peanut or sometimes cocoa or even lemon filling. Nuts for Slovaks are what toffee and caramel are for British - we eat them in biscuits, cakes, chocolate and even pasta.
  • Poppy:
    No, no that poppy! OK, old wives' tales say mother used to add poppy to toddlers' milk to help them sleep. Same as nuts, ground poppy seeds are very popular ingredient for baking, often combined with cherries.
